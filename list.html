The Highest Quality Shit

  • Worm; Worm is a 1.7mil word web-serial written by author Wildbow.
    Worm is about a young parahuman with the power to control bugs. On her first night out as a superhero, Taylor Hebert takes down a notorious supervillian, and causes an unintentional power vacuum. The dominos fall where they may.
    Direct message me on Discord [minish#6273] (or ping me in #read) if you want an e-book.

  • Ever17; Ever17 is a 420k word visual novel written by Kotaro Uchikoshi. (he made zero escape)
    Ever17 is about a group of people who are trapped in the underwater theme park LeMU, due to A Series of Unfortunate Events. They have 119 hours to escape, and Shit Happens.
    The PC port is abandonware; download the (previously linked) modded version for a better time.
    Also, use my (edited) spoiler-free guide to go through the early routes. (and also to not accidentally bump yourself off the true ending)

  • Yume Nikki; Yume Nikki is a ~8 hour explorative RPGMaker game created by Kikiyama.
    Yume Nikki is about traversing the dreamscape of Madotsuki. It has an ending.
    Yume Nikki now has an official English translation, and has been published on Steam by Playism 14 years after the original release.
    Try to play blind; I might write a spoiler-free guide for collecting the effects at some point, but it's still best to try to do it yourself.
    Also, when you've finished, check out Dreamstuck! It's an mspaventure made by Shadok123 (very cool dude) and it's very good.

High Quality (free) Literature

  • The Library Unpublished; The Library Unpublished is a 34k word web-novel written by author al_fa.
    The Library Unpublished is about a bookworm and a book thief discovering a book that doesn't exist.
    The plot of The Northern Caves is entirely spoiled in this, so it's recommended you read it before you start. Or don't, I suppose.

  • Modern Cannibals; Modern Cannibals is a 111k word web novel written by author Bavitz.
    Modern Cannibals is about learning to cut out the toxicity in your life by flinging it off a metaphorical rooftop.
    First several chapters usually give off a bad impression, but it gets good after that.

  • The Nonary Games; Nonary Games is a ~50 hour bundle of puzzle games/visual novels 999 and VLR, both written by Kotaro Uchikoshi. (he made ever17)
    Nine Hours, Nine Persons, Nine Doors is about nine individuals trapped in a large ship by an unknown person known as Zero. They have nine hours to find the number nine door and escape, before the ship sinks.
    Virtue's Last Reward is about nine individuals trapped in a large facility by an unknown person known as Zero. They are forced to participate in the Ambidex Game, in which the players are forced to Ally or Betray.
    There is a third and final installment, Zero Time Dilemma, available separately.

  • A Beginner's Guide to the End of the Universe; AGBttEotU is a 780-page mspaventure made by Crippledvulture.
    A Beginner's Guide to the End of the Universe is about Everyman and his growing entourage of allies treking through the endless white void that surrounds them to find answers and/or a way to survive.
    There's also a sequel (Chairman Jack: Emerge) but it's pretty confusing, the images are dead, and it has also been on indefinite hiatus since March 2012.

  • Kid Radd; Kid Radd is a 601-page flash-based webcomic made by Dan Miller.
    Kid Radd is about the titular character and company being taken out of their game by the Moderators, and being forced to become productive members of the society of their server, Hopetown. Some things go wrong.
    Kid Radd starts off pretty slow, but gets better.

High Quality Video Games

  • Beglitched; Beglitched is a ~10 hour puzzle game made by Hexecutable.
    Beglitched is about you discovering the mysterious Glitch Witch's (manipulates the fabric of the modern world to her will. kind of a big deal) laptop, and running her errands.
    Fun fact: I am (as of writing this) one of only 2889 people who own this game. That's not even accounting for how many have even finished it.
    As such, there are no guides. Anywhere. Just you and Beglitched's cyberpink minesweeping. Have fun!

  • Tower of Heaven; Tower of Heaven is a ~30 minute free browser platforming game developed by Askiisoft.
    Tower of Heaven is about reaching for the heavens.
    It's a short and satisfying flash-based game. The soundtrack is available here.

  • Cave Story; Cave Story is a ~8 hour metriodvania game developed entirely by Pixel from 1999 to 2004.
    Cave Story is about a scrappy amnesiac waking up in a cave, finding a gun, and saving a race of rabbit people, with the extension of shooting a lot of things. It's really good.
    The path for the true ending is stupidly complicated; backup a save from the beginning of Labyrinth (if you can), and read this guide after you've gotten the normal ending. (DO NOT READ IF YOU HAVEN'T)
    Also, Cave Story has a metric fuckton of ports, in addition to the original (free) version.
    There's the Steam remake, and ports for the Wii, DSi, two versions for the 3DS, and a remake for the Switch.

  • OneShot; OneShot is a ~6 hour puzzle game, made by Little Cat Feet.
    OneShot is about guiding young Niko through a decaying world. The world knows you exist. You only have one shot - wait actually you have two.
    The game is mostly puzzles in the form of fetch quests, with some cool 'meta puzzles' sparsed inbetween. It's good, but the story is flawed, retrospectively. I like it, though.

  • The Danganronpa Series; Danganronpa is a ~100+ hour series of Pheonix Wright-esque games. (with one canon spinoff and one canon anime)
    • Danganronpa: Trigger Happy Havoc is about 15 high-school students trapped inside of Hope's Peak Academy, where to escape you have to come up with a really complicated murder plot.
      If you haven't been spoiled on this game, you are one of three humans left alive with the ability to go in blind.
    • Danganronpa 2: Goodbye Despair is about 16 high-school students trapped on an island resort, where to escape you have to come up with a really complicated murder plot.
      It's the best one.
    • Danganronpa Another Episode: Ultra Despair Girls is about 2 people trapped in a city infested with murder robots and murder children, and it HAS AN ACTUAL RAPE MINIGAME WHO THOUGHT THIS WAS A GOOD IDEA IN A CANON DANGANRONPA GAME
    • Danganronpa 3: End of Hope's Peak Academy is not worth your time
    • Danganronpa V3: Killing Harmony is about 16 high-school students trapped inside of the Ultimate Academy for Gifted Juveniles, where to escape you have to come up with a really complicated murder plot.
      It has a fun and possibly anger-inducing twist.
